Staying Safe Online

Why is online safety important

Between 2023 and 2024, Cyber attacks across the globe has seen an increase by around 30%. The losses made from cyber crime are expected to hit 24 trillion dollars by 2027, if cyber crime continues to exponentially increase at the rate it currently is. It can be extremely harmful

Protection of Personal information

Additionally, because we live more of our lives through our screens the more personal details and information we keep on the digital landscape. This places us into an increasing level of risk when this information ends up in the wrong hands. For example information such as: personal communications, financial transactions, health records and past social media posts are all components of what is known as your digital footprint. All of this information can be traced back to you and can be utilised in many ways, sometimes malicously.

Protection of children

Children are the most vunerable group of internet users due to them being unable to recognise potential risks. This maeks them targets for cybercriminals and online theats. Around 25% of 5-7 year olds have a smart phone, and 76% of them will use a tablet. 32% of parents have also stated that they would let there child use social media entirely on their own. These statistics are taken from studies conducted between 2017 to present day.
Furthermore, with the rise of social media, there has been an increase in cyberbullying and online harrassment. 73% of young women have recieved unwanted sexual content online. 26.5% of American teenagers have experienced cyberbullying within the last 30 days. These statistics clearly indicate that online crime is a hugely widespread problem and steps should be taken to try and protect people online.

Social Engineering

Social engineering in the context of cyber security is the process of maniupulating and deceiving someone into giving sensitive information or control over a computer system. Social engineering is done entirely in person and therefore relies on human interaction and psycology to work, it cannot be defended against using software. Therefore it is vital people can understand what social engineering is and how harmful it can be.

98% of cyber attacks will involve some sort of social engieering 75% of companies have reported being a victim of a socially engineered attack in the year 2020.

Practices for staying safe online

Securing Connections

1. Only connect to internet connections you know are secure. Especially when handling sensitive information. You can tell if a webpage is secure if it has HTTPS in the URL.
Some good practices to use when connecting to networks you are not familiar with is using a VPN (virtual private network) for added security via encryption.

2. Disabling bluetooth on devices with very private information can prevent hacking. However this is most likely overkill however it is something to keep in mind

Protecting Devices and Accounts

Secure passwords

1. One of the best ways to ensure that your devices and accounts are secure from outside attack is to use secure unique passwords for everything. If your the forgetful type, a password manager can be extremely useful for keeping track and making sure you dont forget. A great resource to check if your password is secure is to use https://www.passwordmonster.com/

Keeping software updated

2. Additionally, all software should be updated to the latest possible version. This is because software engineer will include security enhancements and patches to bugs that hackers may attempt to exploit. This greatly reduces the chance that your device or account will be a target.

Encryption

3. Using only fully encrypted communication channels, especially when exchaning private or sensitive information is extremely important. Encryption is the process of protecting data by scrambling and altering that data into a format that is unreadable by humans. This is done using whats known as keys. Keys are essentially the algorithm that is resonsible for scrambling the data, therefore the data can only be read by individual who possess the encryption key. This means that outside hackers who may attempt to intercept your message that you are sending cannot physically read it as its simply a bunch of jumbled up nonsense. For this reason I highly suggest double checking messaging services or websites where sensitive data is being exhanged to make sure that it is encrypted. You can again check this by ensuring that the URL contains HTTPS.
